Try a pretend password

Use a made-up example that is not connected to you or your accounts. Try invented word chunks, not real names or common words.

Start typing to test strength
  • At least 12 characters
  • Uppercase and lowercase letters
  • A number
  • A symbol
  • Hard to guess from your life
  • Avoids common dictionary words

Better than a word

Dictionary words such as football, dragon, or icecream are easier for attackers to guess, even with a number added.

Use invented chunks

Make a pronounceable nonsense phrase such as vexa-nimble-tovu, then add capitals, numbers, and a symbol.

Never reveal real ones

For class activities, only use pretend examples. A real password should stay private, even from friends.
